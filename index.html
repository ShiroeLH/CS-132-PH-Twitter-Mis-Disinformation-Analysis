Here's an overview of our project.

This project aims to look over the prevalence of red-tagging of students on social media by scraping Twitter posts from different accounts aiming to falsely identify state university students as members of the CPP-NPA-NDF. This project also quantitatively explores the extent to which Philippine Twitter accounts propagate this mis/disinformation.

Motivation

Freedom of expression, the right to life and security, and several human rights are all violated by the practice of "red tagging" (Khattab, 2022). The Philippine government should take action to put an end to "red tagging" and make sure that students are not targeted based on their political beliefs or expressing their sentiments and opinions.

Problem

Red tagging of students in the Philippines has become a serious concern, as many student organizations and individuals have been baselessly accused of being communists or terrorists. Red-tagging can lead to danger, as the Commission on Human Rights has frequently stated, noting that many people who were Red-tagged ultimately ended up being "killed, injured, illegally arrested, charged with trumped-up cases, or otherwise put in harm's way" (Burgos, 2022).

Solution

The purpose of this project is to contribute evidences that the Red-tagging of students really exists and is prevalent in social media platforms, specifically Twitter. This will increase awareness and hopefully reduce instances of Red-tagging that happen within the country specifically to students in universities and colleges.
