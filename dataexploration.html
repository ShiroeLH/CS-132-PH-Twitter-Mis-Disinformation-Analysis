Tokenization and lower casing

Using WordCloud, the tweets is divided into individual words or tokens, which is then analyzed separately.Additionally, lowercasing is applied to ensure consistency and treat words with different capitalizations as the same. To determine which colleges and universities are most targeted in the red tag-related tweets, a dictionary is created. This dictionary consists of the acronyms or abbreviations of various colleges and universities, with their corresponding full names as the values. The dictionary is created in lowercase to match the lowercase tokens. The frequency of occurrence of each college or university is tracked by checking how many times their lowercase representation appears in the tokenized tweets. By analyzing the frequency of these colleges and universities in the tokens, it becomes possible to identify which ones are most frequently mentioned or targeted in the red tag-related tweets.

Stop words are removed to improve analysis or modeling efficiency. WordCloud library provides a predefined set of English stop words like "and," "the," and "in" that are frequently used but don't add much context. These words are eliminated from the dataset to prioritize more important terms and reduce noise. The word "up" is manually excluded from the English stop words to prevent mistakenly removing instances of "UP" referring to the University of the Philippines. Filipino stop words are also considered. The words were sourced from the stopwords-tl repository on GitHub. By eliminating both English and Filipino stop words, the analysis focuses on more meaningful and informative terms found in red tag-related tweets about colleges and universities.

Interpolation

To aggregate the data, the date and time is resampled to a monthly frequency and then the mean value is calculated for each month. This interpolation method using interpolate()fills in any missing values or smooths out irregularities in the data by fitting a spline curve through the existing data points.

To simplify and organize continuous date and time data, it was divided into Monthly, Yearly intervals or bins by using groupby(). A specific use of this collecting the number of tweets per month using ...groupby(pd.Grouper(key="Date posted", freq="1M")).count(), and collecting number and mean of engagements per year ...groupby(pd.Grouper(key="Date posted", freq="1Y")).sum() and ...groupby(pd.Grouper(key="Date posted", freq="1Y")).mean()

VISUALIZATION

Various visualization techniques are employed to gain insights from the data and communicate findings effectively. Some common visualization methods include:

Scatterplots/histograms

These plots show the distribution and relationship between two continuous variables. Scatterplots display the relationship between variables, while histograms illustrate the distribution of a single variable.:

This scatterplot display the relationship between the number of retweets and the length of the tweet.

This scatterplot display the relationship between the content type and the number of engagements of the tweet

Heat maps

Heat maps provide a visual representation of the correlation between different features. This is particularly useful for identifying patterns or relationships between variables in a tabular format.

Visual representation of the correlation between number of likes, replies, and retweets, and length of tweet

Bar/swarm/violin plots

These plots are used to compare categorical variables or visualize the distribution of a continuous variable across different categories. Bar plots display the frequency or count of each category, while swarm plots show individual data points along with the distribution. Violin plots combine a box plot and a kernel density plot to display the distribution of data.

This bar plot display the frequency of each length of tweets

This Word Cloud serves as a visual representation of the frequency of words within the gathered tweets

Line graphs

Line graphs are commonly used for time series analysis to visualize the trend or pattern of a variable over time. They show the relationship between two continuous variables with time on the x-axis.

This line graph or time series graph shows frequency of tweets over the given time period

Each plot in the visualization stage should have a title or caption that clearly describes the purpose or key finding. Axes labels with units of measurement should be included to provide context for the variables being plotted. Legends are used when multiple colors, markers, or sizes are employed to differentiate different categories or groups in the plot.

FEATURE ANALYSIS

Features within a dataset were examined to gain insights and make informed decisions about their relevance and usefulness for the topic.

Feature generation

School and Tweet Length features based on existing data were added to help in further analysis. The "School" (df_clean['school']) feature was generated by extracting values from the tweets using a manually created dictionary of college acronyms and related words. The "Tweet Length" (df_clean['Length']) feature represents the length of each tweet, providing information about the number of characters or words used in the tweet.
